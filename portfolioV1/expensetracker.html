As part of a 2022 New Years resolution, I decided to take it into my own hands by building a simple Expense Tracking app that allowed the user to simply track their expenditure using Swift. This empowered userâ€™s to become more financially literate without having to pay for premium subscriptions on existing apps on the app store.

Problem Space

Existing apps on the Apple App Store offer freemium models that discourage customisability and leave users to operate within preset categories.

There are two major problem themes identified in the current experience:
  1. Users are less likely to remain consistent with expense tracking due to the unnecessary complexity of categories and expense input
  2. Misalignment with intent and app principles where users are encouraged to spend more money in order to manage their finances

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is composed of three underlying capabilities:
  • A free solution that aligns the financial incentives and underlying motivations of using the app
  • A simple app that optimises for repeat and sustained use to encourage healthy financial accountability habits
  • A useful app that drives both accountability and insight into financial spending habits, allowing users to customise spending categories to better align with financial goals
